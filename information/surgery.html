ACL Reconstruction

ACL reconstruction is a type of surgery done to replace a torn anterior cruciate ligament (ACL), which is a major ligament in your knee. The torn ligament is removed and replaced by a ligament from other parts of your knee or a deceased donor, and it is performed through small incisions around your knee.

This injury commonly happens from sudden stops and changes in movement during sports activities such as basketball, soccer, and football.

Outcomes of successful ACL surgery include:

  • Return to normal sports activities and ADLs
  • Protection to future damage of the knee cartilage
  • Normal functioning knee

Knee Arthroscopy

Arthroscopy is a minimally invasive procedure that allows doctors to have a clear visualisation of your knee condition and come up with an appropriate diagnosis. It is also performed to treat joint injuries through small incisions in your skin.

As a minimally invasive procedure, arthroscopy provides you with many benefits over traditional surgery. This includes:

  • Smaller incisions
  • Less scarring
  • Less bleeding during surgery
  • Muscles and tendons remain intact
  • Faster recovery
  • More comfortable rehabilitation

Total Knee Replacement

Total knee replacement, also called knee arthroplasty or knee replacement, is a surgical procedure to replace the joint (i.e. bottom end of the femur and the top end of the tibia) with an artificial joint or a prosthesis. After this surgical procedure, you can expect easier and painless movement on your daily activities.

Who can undergo total knee replacement?

Patients who have arthritis and experience pain and stiffness on their knee that causes the following:

  • Inability to perform usual sports activities
  • Difficulty in workouts and trainings
  • Interference with your leisure activities
  • Constant discomfort despite taking non-operative measures such as exercise and medications

The pain worsened in the mornings or due to strenuous activities is one of the most definitive signs of osteoarthritis.
